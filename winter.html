Winter





Why did I want
To lie on ice with you
Maybe it was the frost
a crack in time
We went missing
In a snowflake

-Milene Minssieux
Circles in the Snow

I drew circles in the snow
With ink from the moon
Nude
To a january breeze
Lunar letters drip down my thighs
Dyeing winter's skin

A glowing torch
As if held by a slender arm
Hovers in the night
Infusing the canvas
With its flickering gleam
Carmine and crimson strokes

Spill

Each woman's pain
Each woman's name

Slushed in sanguine tears
My feet advance over crisp soils
Mending generations of calloused soles
I step in circles with my heels in the snow

-Milene Minssieux

Dust

Sometimes
it's evanescent
Sometimes
It's cold
And rests on your forehead
like a snowflake
It can wander in a breeze
Never quite finding its way to the ground
It can linger in your hair
Or land in your heart
And crystallize
Binding thoughts to stones
Unfolding its faces
Like a lunar landscape

-Milene Minssieux
