Summer





The breeze folds us
The earth holds us
The sky stirs us
The moon humbles us
Let us allow our eyes
And senses
To bear of our lives
Our hearts to bear our dreams
Our hands to bear each other

-Milene Minssieux

Indigo Suns

The fridge was empty
So I found plums
And pricked them
With the tips of my fingers
I saw five indigo suns
Worlds wavered in front of me
Imbibed in summer days and light
The moisture dripped from my hands
And ripened on the floor
Dribbling sapid verses
In the wooden planks
I opened my mouth
And swallowed a sun
Its blue glaze shedding in my throat
Sour rivers leaked into a gorge
Filling cracks
And my cheeks
Kernels floated adrift
Spreading in the valley
Waiting for their turn to sink
And settle in the rocky soil
Of a body

-Milene Minssieux
My words

I would have left you my words
Because this is
How I love
I craved to give
Letters soaked in rivers
And summer rain
Lyrics resting in orchids
Verses slipping in sap
And worlds carried by dust
I would have spread these words
Like satin sheets
Over your sailing mind
When you were close to sleep
Because this is
How I love
I allowed these worlds of wonder
To simmer in my mind
And watched these weavings unfurl
Carving them into paper and ink
-This is
How I love myself

-Milene Minssieux
