Spring





The tint of sand
Marks my skin
The chant of roses
Stains my blood
Spring's verdant foliage
Binds my heart
In a lust for life
What a delight
To live as an offspring
Of this Earth

-Milene Minssieux
Lungs and white flowers

don't give me tools
or make my hands move
let me sit
a while longer
So the morning light

can slip through me
shadows stain my chest
These are the dents
Where rain gathers
into yearning
A white rose
Swelling
When I breathe
Filling my lungs
with an audacity to create
Prose reveres the glory
Of an evanescent spring
These words
Will last

Unlike this coat
I have borrowed
While breath binds
This mind to matter
A splendid imposter
Of herbage and spirit

I can burn this page
Set flames to these words
Lest the embers
find the earth
and mingle with the soil

So when we wake
A sprout rises
                stretching
                   to the light

-Milene Minssieux
Things we Need

All the broken hearts
Of this sleepless city
If we could just
Find each other
And give ourselves
A great big hug
Let the night close our lids
Folded together
Like a cloud of stardust
And awaken in Spring
Dressed in white
By a pool
Filled in lily pads
River songs
Mermaids and swans

-Milene Minssieux
